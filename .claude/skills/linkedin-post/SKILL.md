---
name: linkedin-post
description: Write a LinkedIn post from a topic, idea, or article URL. Matches the user's voice with a strong hook, punchy body, and clear CTA. Use when the user wants to post on LinkedIn or turn something into a LinkedIn post.
---

Ask the user:
1. "What's the topic, idea, or URL you want to post about?"
2. "What's your goal for this post — build audience, drive clicks, share a lesson, or something else?"
3. (Optional) "Paste 1–2 examples of your past LinkedIn posts so I can match your voice."

Then write 2 versions of the post:

---

## Version A — Story-led
Opens with a specific moment or observation. Builds to a lesson. Ends with a question or soft CTA.

## Version B — Insight-led
Opens with the counterintuitive claim. Backs it up with 3 short supporting points. Ends with a clear takeaway.

---

**Rules for both versions:**
- First line must work as a standalone hook — the reader decides to expand based on that line alone
- No "I'm excited to share…" or "Thrilled to announce…"
- Line breaks every 1–2 sentences (LinkedIn format)
- 150–250 words max
- End with one question or CTA, not both
- 3–5 relevant hashtags at the bottom

Ask: "Which version do you prefer? I can adjust tone or punch up the hook."
