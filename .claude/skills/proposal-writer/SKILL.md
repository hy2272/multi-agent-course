---
name: proposal-writer
description: Write a structured client or internal proposal from a brief. Covers problem, solution, timeline, investment, and next steps. Use when the user needs to send a proposal, pitch a project, or document a plan for approval.
---

Ask the user:
1. "Briefly describe what you're proposing and to whom."
2. "What problem does this solve for the recipient?"
3. "What's the rough scope, cost, and timeline?"
4. "What's the desired outcome — approval, a meeting, a signature?"

Then produce:

## Proposal: [Title]

**Prepared for:** [Client / Team / Stakeholder]
**Date:** [Today]
**Prepared by:** [User's name if given]

---

### The problem we're solving
2–3 sentences on the specific challenge the recipient faces. Use their language, not yours. Make them feel understood before you pitch anything.

### Our proposed solution
What you're proposing, in plain language. What it is, what it does, and how it specifically addresses the problem above.

### How it works
3–5 steps or phases. Enough detail to be credible, not so much that it becomes a spec document.

| Phase | What happens | Timeline |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| [1] | [description] | [e.g. Week 1–2] |

### Investment
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| [component] | $[X] |
| **Total** | **$[Y]** |

Include what's in scope and — critically — what's out of scope.

### Why us / why now
One short paragraph. What makes you the right choice, and why acting now matters.

### Next steps
Three concrete steps with owners and dates. Make the first step easy to say yes to.

---

Tone: confident, not salesy. Proposals that sound like brochures don't get signed.
